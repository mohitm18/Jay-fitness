The Science of Strength

Strength training is more than just lifting weights; it's about neurological adaptation and muscle fiber recruitment. By focusing on compound movements like squats and deadlifts, you trigger a hormonal response that boosts metabolism for up to 48 hours.

  • Hypertrophy Focus: Stimulating muscle growth through tension.
  • CNS Gains: Improving brain-to-muscle connection.
  • Bone Density: Strengthening the skeletal structure.

Endurance & Heart Health

Cardiovascular training is the engine of your performance. Whether it's HIIT or steady-state cardio, improving your VO2 Max ensures that your muscles receive oxygenated blood faster, allowing you to train harder and recover in record time.

  • VO2 Max: Increasing the peak oxygen consumption.
  • Fat Oxidation: Training the body to use fat as fuel.
  • Recovery Rate: Lowering resting heart rate for longevity.
